Geodemographic Classification of Lisbon

The objective of this project is to develop a geodemographic classification for the city of Lisbon. This classification aims to assist in understanding the basic aspects of geodemographic classifications, which can be seen as a specific case of clustering and segmentation tasks.

Data Provided:

  • Shapefile: A file named lisboa.shp containing the statistical sections of Lisbon.
  • Excel File: A file named (Lisbon_Database.xls) with socio-economic variables corresponding to each statistical section, which can be linked to the shapefile using a code variable.

Tools Used:

  • Microsoft Excel: For data analysis and preprocessing.
  • ArcGIS Pro: For visualizing results and constructing appropriate maps.
  • KNIME: For developing the classification by grouping the data.

Data Description:

The dataset contains 882 records corresponding to the statistical sections of Lisbon, with each record providing information about approximately 600 people and 250 housing units. The data has been partially normalized using ratios to avoid scale effects.
(You can check the variables here)

Steps for Classification:

Understanding the Data:

Create a map to identify patterns in the spatial distribution of residents aged 65 or older in Lisbon.

Figure 1 - Spatial distribution of residents aged 65 or older

1. The central areas have a higher population density of elderly residents.

2. Larger areas with lower population density, such as Parque Florestal de Monsanto and Humberto Delgado Airport, demonstrate the influence of land use and the availability of open spaces on demographic distribution.

3. Smaller areas with low elderly population density often coincide with leisure and recreation zones, such as green spaces and sports facilities (e.g., Cidade Universitária), as well as waterfront areas (e.g., Parque das Nações) and industrial zones.

Data Analysis and Cleanup:

Import data into KNIME and clean it by removing empty columns, filtering duplicates, and handling missing values.

Figure 1 - Spatial distribution of residents aged 65 or older

Perform PCA in Python (Click to see the code used) to reduce dimensionality and select variables with high contributions for further analysis.

Determine the optimal number of principal components using the Scree Plot method.

Visualize the loadings of the principal components and identify the variables to retain for clustering.

Figure 4 - PCA Loadings

Clustering:

Use the K-Means algorithm to identify the optimal number of clusters, ranging from 2 to 8, and evaluate the results using the Silhouette Coefficient.
Choose 3 clusters based on the best Silhouette Coefficient scores.

Figure 5 - Silhouette Coefficient

Profiling the Clusters:

Analyze the clusters to understand their main characteristics by calculating averages for different variables.

Cluster 0: Higher proportion of older residents

Cluster 1: Higher proportion of families with young children

Cluster 2: Higher proportion of young professionals

❮ ❯

Cluster 0: Older and retired population, lower educational qualifications, more fluid housing with a tendency towards renting.
Cluster 1: Active adult population with high educational qualifications and stable housing.
Cluster 2: Young population with growing families, facing greater educational and economic challenges, predominantly residing in larger rented accommodations.

Mapping the Results:

Use geographic maps and cluster analyses to understand the specific characteristics of different areas in Lisbon.

Classic residential accommodation with owner-occupier

Classic families with 1 or 2 people

Resident individuals with completed higher education and with only the 1st cycle of basic education completed

Resident women and men aged between 25 and 64

❮ ❯

By following this structured approach, we developed a comprehensive geodemographic classification for Lisbon, providing valuable insights into the socio-economic landscape of the city.
